Workshop Overview

Mobile manipulation, including bimanual coordination, is increasingly combined with foundation models and vision-language-action (VLA) systems. Yet the robotics community still lacks standardized benchmarks that validate these methods in real-world settings. Simulation results often fail to translate to robust deployment due to sim-to-real gaps, platform differences, and limited reproducibility. This workshop proposes a globally coordinated benchmarking initiative through a structured three-phase plan.

Phase I

Open Simulation Benchmark

Release an open simulation benchmark focusing on unstructured manipulation scenarios: cable routing, deformable material handling (thermal pad placement), and caregiving and feeding tasks. Global teams participate through an open call on a shared, reproducible leaderboard.

Phase II

Cross-Continent Real-World Validation

Top simulation entries are validated on real robots at partner labs in Beijing, Hamburg, Munich, and Pittsburgh. Unified hardware protocols and teleoperation pipelines ensure cross-site comparability.

Phase III

IROS Workshop & Roadmap Release

Competition results, invited talks, and a panel discussion are presented at IROS 2026. The workshop releases the benchmark protocol, open dataset, and community roadmap for future iterations.

Key Themes

Four research questions at the heart of the EBiM initiative.

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Closing the gap between simulated training and real-world robot performance.

Cross-Site Reproducibility

Ensuring results transfer across labs, hardware, and operator variation worldwide.

Foundation & VLA Model Evaluation

Rigorous benchmarking of vision-language-action models against real manipulation tasks.

Contact-Rich Manipulation

Tasks demanding precise force control, grasping under uncertainty, and deformable object handling.
