Workshop Overview

Mobile manipulation is rapidly converging with foundation models and vision-language-action (VLA) systems. However, the robotics community still lacks a standardized and reproducible evaluation framework connecting simulation results with real-world deployment. This workshop proposes a globally coordinated benchmarking initiative through a structured three-phase mechanism.

Phase I

Open Simulation Benchmark

Release a curated suite of bimanual manipulation tasks in open simulation environments with standardized APIs. Teams worldwide compete and iterate on a shared, reproducible leaderboard.

Phase II

Cross-Continent Real-World Validation

Top simulation entries are validated on real robots at partner labs in Munich, Beijing, and Pittsburgh. Unified hardware protocols and teleoperation pipelines ensure cross-site comparability.

Phase III

IROS Workshop & Roadmap Release

Findings, lessons, and community consensus are presented at IROS 2026. The workshop concludes with a public release of the benchmark roadmap and open dataset for the broader community.

Key Themes

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Closing the gap between simulated training and real-world robot performance.

Cross-Site Reproducibility

Ensuring results transfer across labs, hardware, and operator variation worldwide.

Foundation & VLA Model Evaluation

Rigorous benchmarking of vision-language-action models against real manipulation tasks.

Contact-Rich Manipulation

Tasks demanding precise force control, grasping under uncertainty, and deformable object handling.

The Benchmark

A multi-axis framework for fair and meaningful evaluation of bimanual manipulation systems.

Current bimanual manipulation benchmarks are fragmented across hardware platforms, task definitions, and evaluation criteria. Our workshop targets the design of a community-wide standard covering task diversity, generalization, robustness, and deployment readiness — evaluated on real robots under standardized conditions.

01

Task Suite

Canonical manipulation tasks spanning assembly, tool use, cloth folding, and food preparation.

02

Metrics

Success rate, precision, cycle time, generalization score, and human-comparative performance.

03

Data Protocol

Shared teleoperation, annotation schema, and hardware calibration guidelines for cross-site compatibility.

04

Hardware Agnosticism

Platform-neutral APIs and sim-to-real transfer tracks to include diverse robot embodiments.

05

Leaderboard

An open, continuously updated leaderboard with verified real-robot submissions.

06

Open Dataset

Curated multi-site demonstration dataset with diverse operators, environments, and objects.

Benchmark Tasks

Six canonical bimanual manipulation tasks spanning contact-rich assembly, deformable object handling, human interaction, and cluttered-scene perception.

Task 01

Cable Routing & Plugging

Route a cable through clamps and plug from Port A to Port B. Requires color/positional understanding, sequential constraint satisfaction, and mobile repositioning.

Contact-Rich Sequential
Task 02

Deformable Sticker Peeling & Placement

Manipulate thermal silica stickers of different colors and shapes. Requires peeling deformable material and precise placement at semantic targets.

Deformable Precision
Task 03

Feeding Task

Autonomous feeding assistance using a spoon to scoop from a bowl of coffee beans. The robot must navigate to a feeding area where a mannequin head is placed at a random position, reach the target region, and deposit beans into an empty collection bowl. Evaluation counts the number of beans successfully transferred.

Human-Centered Safety-Critical
Task 04

Table Cleaning & Setup

Clear, clean, and reset a table with dishes and utensils in correct positions.

Multi-Step Rearrangement
Task 05

Clothes Folding & Sorting

Fold and sort garments by type, requiring deformable object manipulation and category recognition.

Deformable Classification
Task 06

Cluttered Scene Retrieval

Retrieve and sort target objects from cluttered, occluded scenes.

Perception Clutter

Task specifications and evaluation protocols will be released with the simulation benchmark. Detailed task descriptions coming soon.
